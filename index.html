Short Bio

I am currently a Ph.D. candidate in the Department of Computer Science and Engineering at University of Texas at Arlington. My adviser is Dr. Dajiang Zhu. I obtained the B.S. degree and the M.S. degree in Computer Science from Northwestern Polytechnical University in 2015 and 2018, respectively.

My research interests mainly focuses on two major problems:

  • Applying machine learning/deep learning methods to integrate multi-scale and multi-modal brain imaging data for advancing our understanding of the fundamental organization principles of the brain and for gaining insights into various brain disorders, such as Alzheimer's disease and Autism.
  • Leveraging insights from neuroscience to design more effective, efficient, and trustworthy Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) systems (Brain Inspired AGI).
